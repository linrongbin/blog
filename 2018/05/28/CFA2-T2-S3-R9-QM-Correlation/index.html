The candidate should be able to: ### 2.1. Scatter Plots ### 2.2. Correlation Analysis - calculate and interpret a sample covariance and a sample correlation coefficient and interpret a scatter plot; - How to express the relationship between the observations for two data series, or how closely related two data series are? - A scatter plot is a graph. - Correlation coefficient is a single number - The correlation coefficient measures the direction and extent of linear association between two variables - has no unit. - have a maximum value of 1 and a minimum value of −1 - A correlation coefficient of 0 indicates no linear relation between the two variables ### 2.3. Calculating and Interpreting the Correlation Coefficient - sample covariance of two variables (X and Y): \[ Cov_{XY} = \frac {\sum_{i=1}^{n} (X_i - \bar{X})(Y_i - \bar{Y})} {n-1} \] - The correlation coefficient is the covariance of two variables (X and Y) divided by the product of their sample standard deviations (\(s_X\) and \(s_Y\)). \[ r_{XY} = \frac {Cov_{XY}} {s_X \times s_Y} \] - Assumption: the means and variances of X and Y, as well as the covariance of X and Y, are finite and constant.

2.4. Limitations of Correlation Analysis

  • describe limitations to correlation analysis;
    • not always be reliable
      • Two variables can have a strong nonlinear relation and still have a very low correlation
      • outliers are present in one or both of the series
    • correlation does not imply causation
    • spurious correlation
        1. correlation between two variables that reflects chance relationships in a particular data set,
        1. correlation induced by a calculation that mixes each of two variables with a third, and
        1. correlation between two variables arising not from a direct relation between them but from their relation to a third variable.
        • 例子：身高和词汇量的correlation可能是年纪引起的。

2.5 Uses of Correlation Analysis

  • Evaluating Economic Forecasts
  • Style Analysis Correlations (investment portfolios may favor a growth style, a value style)
  • Exchange Rate Return Correlations
  • Correlations among Stock Return Series
  • Correlations of Debt and Equity Returns
  • Correlations among Net Income, Cash Flow from Operations, and Free Cash Flow to the Firm
  • Analysis of Large Datasets—Big Data

2.6. Testing the Significance of the Correlation Coefficient

Significance tests allow us to assess whether apparent relationships between random variables are the result of chance. If we decide that the relationships do not result from chance, we will be inclined to use this information in predictions because a good prediction of one variable will help us predict the other variable. \[ t=\frac {r \sqrt{n-2}} {\sqrt{1-r^2}}\]

formulate a test of the hypothesis that the population correlation coefficient equals zero and determine whether the hypothesis is rejected at a given level of significance;
