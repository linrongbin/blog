Letters for IELTS writing

发表于 2018-07-09 | 更新于 2018-07-10

小作文笔记和例句

（来自《新东方雅思写作套路剖析与范例大全》）

类别：

投诉信，询问信，建议信，求助信，道歉（解释）信，感谢信，邀请信 和 求职信。

格式 （三个部分）

1. 称呼

1.1 对方是机构，且不知名姓：Dear Sir or Madam, 或者 To whom it may concern
1.2 对方是认识，但不熟悉：Dear Mr. (or Ms.) + 姓氏， 例如： Dear Mr. Gates, Dear Ms. Clark
1.3 对方是认识的熟人：Dear + 名字， 例如 Dear John,

2. 主体部分

2.1 开头段（2 句）
2.2 主体段（8～10 句）
2.3 结尾段（2 句）

3. 信末礼貌语和署名

3.1 解释信：Yours faithfully， yours truly
3.2 其他： 有yours sincerely
3.3 很好的朋友：yours， with love and best wishes, lots of love

1/8 投诉信 letter of complaint

  • 当时的情况
  • 具体的问题
  • 可能的后果
  • 自己的要求
  • 对方的补救
  • 建议的措施及时间限制 （未必要严厉）

2/8咨询信 inquiry letter

  • 你需要什么
  • 为什么需要
  • 什么时候要
  • （避免模糊要求）
  • 请求回复 （礼貌）

3/8 求助信 request letter

  • 所需的帮助
  • 为什么需要
  • 说服对方 （Persuade)
  • 答谢对方 （Offer incentives）
  • 时间限制 （deadline）

4/8 道歉信 letter of apology

  • 解释错误 （观点和现实）
  • 不归咎他人
  • 提出解决方法
  • 顾忌对方情绪 -> 建立信任

5/8 建议信 letter to provide info or advice (回信居多)

  • 详细的建议
  • 提供要求的信息
  • 强调重要事项
  • 最重要排第一
  • 乐意进一步帮助

6/8 感谢信 letter of thanks

  • 表示感谢
  • 强调所获帮助的重要性
  • 维持关系

7/8 邀请信 invitation letter

  • 描述活动
  • 指定对象
  • 指定日期，地点，时间
  • 着装要求？
  • 回复要求

8/8 求职信 letter of application

  • 为什么感兴趣
  • 符合工作特点
  • 回复雇主要求
  • 个人背景细节 （教育，经历，才能）-> 体现个人特色
  • 地址和姓名

例句

Letter of complaint

Begining

  1. I am writing to complain about a serious defect found in the computer I bought from you.
  2. I am writing to notify you of a claim for damaged goods against your company.
  3. I am writing to request you to make corrective actions concerning…
  4. I am writing to express my concern / dissatisfaction about …
  5. I would like to draw your attention to …
  6. It has come to my attention that …
  7. A very serious problem has arisen in connection with a language course you provided and operated.
  8. I am writing to request a full refund of the course fee I have paid.
  9. Over the last few weeks I have become increasingly concerned about my neighbour’s erratic habit, burning rubbish in his garden.
  10. I sincerely regret having to write this letter and have been delaying it in hope that things would improve.
  11. I am writing to ask you to replace the CD player I bought from your shop with another in good working order.
  12. I thought I should draw your attention to the situation since…
  13. The devastating effect could be multiplied if this problem cannot be solved immediately.
  14. I am concern that this situation is causing us considerate anxiety.
  15. I would be grateful if you could manage to help me out of the problem

Ending

  1. Unless you take prompt action to correct the car’s defects or refund the purchase price in full, I will be forced to take legal action.
  2. I believe this is the only way to get this matter settled.
  3. Please make the adjustment to my bill.
  4. It is too bad this unfortunate accident occured. Otherwise, I was very pleased with your service.
  5. I will have to refer this matter to my attorney.
  6. I am glad to see what you can do to rectify this situation.
  7. I urge you to reconsider your consideration.
  8. I would be grateful if you would ensure that the same thing does not happen again.
  9. If you could find time to let us know, it would set our minds at rest.
  10. I am reluctant to take the matter up elsewhere and hope that you will be able to let me have some explaination of the incident.
  11. A time for further discussion on this issue can be arranged either by telephoning me at 12346789 or wiriting to me via the address…
  12. I hope we can work something out to our mutual benefit.
  13. Your agreement to the suggested course of action will be appreciated.
  14. Your comments will be appreciated.
  15. I would like to discuss this matter with you further. Please contact me at 12345678. I look forward to resoving this matter with you.
  16. I believe this matter can be resolved quickly and look forward to your early reply.
  17. Please let me know what you propose in relation to this issue as soon as possible.
  18. I am sorry to write to you in this manner and I hope that you will not be offended.
  19. If this matter is not put right soon, I fear it could have serious consequences.
  20. I await your reply and trust that it will contain an explaination taht some mistakes have been made by your company.
  21. I would like to stress that I will not tolerate a situation like this.
  22. I trust that you will be able to correct these matters without delay and that no further action will be necessary on my part.
  23. I understand that my rights are covered by laws and I am looking to your company for a return of the price paid within 14 days.
  24. I would be glad of a reply without further delay, or I may have to consider legal action.
  25. I am wondering if it would be possible for us to meet and discuss the situation.
  26. I hope we can meet and get it sorted out very soon.
  27. I trust that we can resolve this matter quickly and amicably.
  28. If I do not hear from you within the next sever days, I will have no option but to go to the city councile to have my complaint heard.
  29. I am sure you will understand that this problem is causing me considerabel inconvenience and expense, and I am anxious to have it settled as soon as possible.
  30. I look forward to receiving a replacement as soon as possible.
  31. I think that 300 dollars would be a reasonable sum of compensation and look forward to receive yur cheque.
  32. If it happens again I will commence legal action without further notice.

Inquiry letter

  1. I am writing to enquire whether I may become a member of your club.
  2. I would be grateful if you could let me know your annual fee charges and any other information which would be useful.
  3. Please let me know as soon as possibel how you propose to settle this matter.
  4. You are welcome to call in or to telephone me at xxxxxxx.
  5. Please contact me on xxxxx if you would like to talk about it further.
  6. Thank you for your attention.
  7. Please let me know as soon as possible whether or not you will accept this offer.
  8. Although at this time of year you will have more work to deal with than usual, I would appreciate a reply in a not too distant future.

Request Letter

  1. I would like to make an appointment to speak with you about my wallet I lost on the flight AUS123.
  2. I would like to arrange an appointment for next Friday (15 October) at four fifteen.
  3. I would be grateful if you could give any assistance.
  4. I would be most grateful if you could give any instructions.
  5. I would be very grateful for any advice you can offer.
  6. I do hope you will be able to send me a favourable reply and I look forward to hearing from you in due course.
  7. I would be most grateful if you would intervene in this matter.
  8. I hope you will consider my request favourably.

Letter of apology

  1. I just wanted to write you a quick note to apologise for not being able to keep our appointment tomorrow.
  2. In deference to your valuable time, I would like to get straight to the point and admit that I was wrong.
  3. I just really hope that you will be able to accept my apology.
  4. Please accept my apologies for …
  5. Please accept my sincere apology for missing the interview scheduled for…
  6. Please accept my apology for the delay and thank you for your understanding.
  7. I will do everything in my power to ensure that this type of error does not occur again.

  8. Although I make every attempt to insure that …, this unfortunate incident can occasionally occur.

  9. Letter to provide information or advice
  10. If I can be of any assistance in any way, please donot hesitate to call on me.
  11. I am available at Flat 304, Green Revenue, St. Eden if you require any further information
  12. I am happy to supply any further information you may require and I look forward to hearing from you shortly.

  13. Please contact me if I can be of any assistance.

Letter of thanks

  1. I just wanted to send a note to say how much I appreciate the help you have given me.
  2. I am writing to thank you for permitting us to use your resources.
  3. With very best wishes and thanks.
  4. I appreciate the support you have provided and your assistance has been invaluable to me.

Invitation letter

  1. Perhaps you could give us a call or write to us to let us know what time you expect to arrive.
  2. I thank you in anticipation of your arrival.
  3. I am so pleased that you ware planning to drop by.
  4. I would like to offer you…
  5. I would like to invite you to dinner and be our guest.
  6. I would like to invite yu to join us and attend this meeting.
  7. I am glad to invite you to participate in my graduation ceremony.
  8. I am honoured to invite you to our wedding, as I understand that you only come to visit on special occations.

Letter of application

  1. Further to your advertisement in yesterday’s Washington Herald for a senior PA, I would very much like to be considered for this vacancy.
  2. I am writing with regard to your vacancy for a language tutor.
  3. I was most interested to read your advertisement for a bookkeeper and feel I could be just the person for your vacancy.
  4. I write to enquire if you have an opening for a sales representative.
  5. I do hope that I will be successful, and that I may hear from you in the near future.
  6. I do hope that I may be considered for the job and that you may invite me for interview in the near future.
