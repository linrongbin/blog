Letters for IELTS writing

发表于 2018-07-09 | 更新于 2018-07-10

小作文笔记和例句

（来自《新东方雅思写作套路剖析与范例大全》）

类别：

投诉信，询问信，建议信，求助信，道歉（解释）信，感谢信，邀请信 和 求职信。

格式 （三个部分）

1. 称呼

1.1 对方是机构，且不知名姓：Dear Sir or Madam, 或者 To whom it may concern
1.2 对方是认识，但不熟悉：Dear Mr. (or Ms.) + 姓氏， 例如： Dear Mr. Gates, Dear Ms. Clark
1.3 对方是认识的熟人：Dear + 名字， 例如 Dear John,

2. 主体部分

2.1 开头段（2 句）
2.2 主体段（8～10 句）
2.3 结尾段（2 句）

3. 信末礼貌语和署名

3.1 解释信：Yours faithfully， yours truly
3.2 其他： 有yours sincerely
3.3 很好的朋友：yours， with love and best wishes, lots of love

1/8 投诉信 letter of complaint

  • 当时的情况
  • 具体的问题
  • 可能的后果
  • 自己的要求
  • 对方的补救
  • 建议的措施及时间限制 （未必要严厉）

2/8咨询信 inquiry letter

  • 你需要什么
  • 为什么需要
  • 什么时候要
  • （避免模糊要求）
  • 请求回复 （礼貌）

3/8 求助信 request letter

  • 所需的帮助
  • 为什么需要
  • 说服对方 （Persuade)
  • 答谢对方 （Offer incentives）
  • 时间限制 （deadline）

4/8 道歉信 letter of apology

  • 解释错误 （观点和现实）
  • 不归咎他人
  • 提出解决方法
  • 顾忌对方情绪 -> 建立信任

5/8 建议信 letter to provide info or advice (回信居多)

  • 详细的建议
  • 提供要求的信息
  • 强调重要事项
  • 最重要排第一
  • 乐意进一步帮助

6/8 感谢信 letter of thanks

  • 表示感谢
  • 强调所获帮助的重要性
  • 维持关系

7/8 邀请信 invitation letter

  • 描述活动
  • 指定对象
  • 指定日期，地点，时间
  • 着装要求？
  • 回复要求

8/8 求职信 letter of application

  • 为什么感兴趣
  • 符合工作特点
  • 回复雇主要求
  • 个人背景细节 （教育，经历，才能）-> 体现个人特色
  • 地址和姓名

为什么样本方差除以n-1

发表于 2018-05-28

除以n-1是为了实现无偏估计，但是为什么呢？

问题转为证明：

样本方差的期望等于总体方差。 \[ E(s^2) = E\left(\frac{1}{n - 1} \sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2\right) = \sigma^2 \]

基本公式： \[ \left\{ \begin{aligned} & E(Z_1 + Z_2) = E(Z_1) + E(Z_2), \text{ for any } Z_1, Z_2 \\ & \text{Var}(a Z) = a^2 \text{Var}(Z), \text{ for any } Z \\ & \text{Var}(Z_1 + Z_2) = \text{Var}(Z_1) + \text{Var}(Z_2), \text{ if } Z_1 \text{ and } Z_2 \text{ are independent} \\ \end{aligned} \right. \]

\[ \begin{align} \text{Var}(Z) & = E((Z - E(Z))^2) \\ & = E(Z^2 - 2ZE(Z) + E(Z)^2) \\ & = E(Z^2) - E(2ZE(Z)) + E(Z)^2 \\ & = E(Z^2) - 2E(Z)E(Z) + E(Z)^2 \\ & = E(Z^2) - 2E(Z)^2 + E(Z)^2 \\ & = E(Z^2) - E(Z)^2 \end{align} \]

\[ \begin{aligned} E(s^2) = & \ \frac{1}{n-1} \left[ \sum_{j=1}^{n} E \left( X_j^2 \right) - n E \left(\bar{X}^2 \right) \right] \\ = & \ \frac{1}{n-1} \left[n \sigma^2 + n \mu ^2 - n \left( \frac{1}{n} \sigma^2 + \mu ^2 \right) \right] \\ = & \ \frac{1}{n-1} \left[n \sigma^2 + n \mu ^2 - \sigma^2 - n \mu ^2 \right] \\ = & \ \sigma^2 \\ \end{aligned} \]

\[ \sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \mu)^2 \geq \sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \]

具体的推导过程： \[ \begin{aligned} E\left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \mu)^2\right) = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X} + \bar{X} - \mu)^2\right) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 + \sum_{j=1}^{n} 2(X_j - \bar{X})(\bar{X} - \mu) + \sum_{j=1}^{n} (\bar{X} - \mu)^2 \right) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 + \sum_{j=1}^{n} (\bar{X} - \mu)^2 \right) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \right) + E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (\bar{X} - \mu)^2 \right) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \right) + \sum_{j=1}^{n} E \left((\bar{X} - \mu)^2 \right) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \right) + \sum_{j=1}^{n} \left( \text{Var} (\bar{X} - \mu) + E (\bar{X} - \mu)^2 \right) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \right) + \sum_{j=1}^{n} \left( \text{Var} (\bar{X}) + E (\bar{X} - \mu)^2 \right) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \right) + \sum_{j=1}^{n} \text{Var} (\bar{X}) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \right) + n \text{Var} (\bar{X}) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \right) + n \text{Var} (\frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n} X_j) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \right) + n \frac{1}{n^2} \sum_{j=1}^{n} \text{Var} (X_j) \\ = & \ E \left(\sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - \bar{X})^2 \right) + \sigma^2 \\ \end{aligned} \]

参考： [链接]{!http://mortada.net/computing-sample-variance-why-divide-by-n-1.html}

Correlation Analysis - calculate and interpret a sample covariance and a sample correlation coefficient and interpret a scatter plot; - How to express the relationship between the observations for two data series, or how closely related two data series are? - A scatter plot is a graph. - Correlation coefficient is a single number - The correlation coefficient measures the direction and extent of linear association between two variables - has no unit. - have a maximum value of 1 and a minimum value of −1 - A correlation coefficient of 0 indicates no linear relation between the two variables ### 2.3. Calculating and Interpreting the Correlation Coefficient - sample covariance of two variables (X and Y): \[ Cov_{XY} = \frac {\sum_{i=1}^{n} (X_i - \bar{X})(Y_i - \bar{Y})} {n-1} \] - The correlation coefficient is the covariance of two variables (X and Y) divided by the product of their sample standard deviations (\(s_X\) and \(s_Y\)). \[ r_{XY} = \frac {Cov_{XY}} {s_X \times s_Y} \] - Assumption: the means and variances of X and Y, as well as the covariance of X and Y, are finite and constant.

2.4. Limitations of Correlation Analysis

  • describe limitations to correlation analysis;
    • not always be reliable
      • Two variables can have a strong nonlinear relation and still have a very low correlation
      • outliers are present in one or both of the series
    • correlation does not imply causation
    • spurious correlation
        1. correlation between two variables that reflects chance relationships in a particular data set,
        1. correlation induced by a calculation that mixes each of two variables with a third, and
        1. correlation between two variables arising not from a direct relation between them but from their relation to a third variable.
        • 例子：身高和词汇量的correlation可能是年纪引起的。

2.5 Uses of Correlation Analysis

  • Evaluating Economic Forecasts
  • Style Analysis Correlations (investment portfolios may favor a growth style, a value style)
  • Exchange Rate Return Correlations
  • Correlations among Stock Return Series
  • Correlations of Debt and Equity Returns
  • Correlations among Net Income, Cash Flow from Operations, and Free Cash Flow to the Firm
  • Analysis of Large Datasets—Big Data

Significance tests allow us to assess whether apparent relationships between random variables are the result of chance. 1: The What and Why of GDPR

  • What: General Data Protection Regulation
  • When: Effective Date is May 2018
  • Why: To ensure organisations process personal data in fair, transparent and secure way

2: Personal Data and Processing

  • Personal Data
    • Location
    • Genetic
    • IP address
    • Internet cookies
    • Names
    • Addresses
    • Phone Numbers
    • Papers
    • Photographs
  • Sensitive Data
    • Race or ethnic origin
    • Political opinion
    • Religious belifs
    • Physical conditions
    • Mental health conditions
    • Sexual orientation
    • Biometric data
    • Genetic data
    • Trade union memberships
    • Financial information (!Not Included)
  • Personal Data Processing

3: The Key Principles

  • Fair, Lawful and Transparent
    • Consent (OPT-IN)
  • Data Minimisation (Only hold what data we needed)
  • Data Retention (As long as neccessary)
  • Purpose Limitation
  • Accuracy
  • Data Security (Protect data from lost, damaged and stolen)

4: Individual’s Rights

  • To be informed
  • Access
  • Rectification
  • To be forgotten or erasure
  • To restrict processing (Our organization & Third Party)
  • Data portability (Eg. Training Records for own use)
  • To object (Eg. Unsubcribe Marketing emails)
  • Automated decision making and profiling

5: Data Protection Breaches

  • When?
    • Data is accidentally or unlawfully destroyed (Lost or Altered)
    • Disclosed or accessed without authorisation
  • How long to report (72 hours)
